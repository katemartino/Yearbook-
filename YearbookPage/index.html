RAIN or SHINE

The STUCO Spring Carnival is carried out despite inclement weather

STEP RIGHT UP BC students step outside in the front of school to enjoy the Student co's annual festival.

An event plaged by rain the year before, this years Student Council Carnival was again threatened to be reluctantly moved indoors. But luckily the weather cooperated and the event was held out on the front lawn. “This was the first year the carnival was outside - we tried to do it outside last year but it rained. I think it went really well. We were contemplating on moving it inside but then we wouldn’t have been able to tie dye and have some of the inflatables,” said Rachel Kolb (‘16), a co-chair of the event. Made enjoyable by the inclusion of most of the clubs, students were able to walk from booth to booth and participate in activities put on by their fellow students. Jessica Olson (‘18) remembered “activities like dunking teachers in a tank, tie-dye, live music, and they gave away cotton candy and it was actually pretty good.” Evident by the line of students spanning across the parking lot, the free cotton candy was a hit with the students.
Perhaps unsurprisingly, the most popular event was the tie-dye spring fling t-shirts hosted by student council. “I think tie dying was also a huge success because you could create your own shirt and personalize it,also student council has never done anything like that before so it was different. Just a few booths down was the Red Kettle Club table, where club treasurer Lucy Kwiatkowski (‘16) was handing out snow cones, “We went with red snow cones because it’s Red Kettle Club.” The group was attempting to draw in more members, and strangely enough, meeting attendance doubled the next week.
